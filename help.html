Getting started
General

Thank you for installing iBees. iBees consists of a hub, with up to 5 wireless scales connected to it. A scale can measure hive weights from 5 to 100kg, with 100gr accuracy. Once setup it will run fully automatic. At the end of every day, it will upload its readings to your personal web page. There, you can follow up the weight progress of your hives. Various SMS alerts can be armed by sending a simple SMS.

SIM card

iBees uploads its data through a GPRS mobile datalink. It requires a SIM card with an active SMS and data subscription. The system uses a univeral PIN (9876). Before inserting the SIM card into the iBees hub, insert it first in your personal phone. Change its issued PIN to 9876 and test network connection. A daily upload will only use about 20kB of data. This is less than 1MB per month!

Hub

Open the plastic casing, insert the SIM-card and slide the power switch to ON. The hub is best placed in the shadow, as it contains a temperature sensor. To establish the best wireless scale connection, avoid having dense obstacles between scales and hub.

Scale

Open the plastic casing, make sure the battery is in place and slide the power switch to ON.

Synchronization

Scales will automatically synchronize with the hub. They periodically wake-up, transmit their data and go back to sleep. The hub aims to upload its stored data every evening around 8pm. If the first upload attempt was unsuccessfull, it will automatically try again after the programmed interval period. When starting up a new hub or replacing its battery's, its internal clock is initially disoriented. The clock will reset automatically. But it can take up to 2 days before the system uploads in the evening.

SMS control
General

iBees hub and scale can be controlled by sending simple SMS messages. When the hub uploads data, it will first check for any newly received messages. You can send an SMS from your personal mobile to iBees any time of the day. This SMS will however only be read at the end of the day. You can arm or disarm a swarm-alert, a theft-alert or a trigger weight. You can change the periodic wake-up interval or contact phone number. You can even get daily weight reports. Caution: the control SMS must be spelled exactly as below! Commands are case-sensitive. A command will only be followed when confirmation SMS has been received. It can take up to a full day for the hub to reply to your SMS.

Setup mobile number

When first setting up iBees, send it a SMS from your mobile phone to let it know your number. This new number will than be used as the only number to communicate with the system. Any SMS's received by any other number will be ignored. Warning: be 100% sure to enter the correct new number! Changes can not be undone! Valid commands are: NewNbr +32478642330 If you wish to later change phone number, you have let iBees know you new number in an SMS send from you old number.

Swarm-Alert

iBees can be programmed to warn you in case of a swarm. It will send out a swarm-alert SMS. When armed it will send you a SMS in case of a sudden weight drop between 1kg and 5kg on one of the scales. When armed it will only be active between 10am and 6pm during the months May, June, July and August. Valid commands are: SwarmAlert ON, SwarmAlert OFF

Theft-Alert

When armed, the system will send a theft-alert SMS in case of a sudden big weight drop between 7pm and 9am. Valid commands are: TheftAlert ON, TheftAlert OFF

Interval

The system periodically wakes-up to measure scale weight, and than goes back to sleep. The wake-up interval can be set at 15min, 30min or 60min. A 30min interval creates 48 datapoints per day. Interval period could for instance be increase during the winter. Valid commands are: Interval 15min, Interval 30min, Interval 60min

Trigger

Any iBees scale can be individually programmed to send you a SMS when its weight drops below a certain value. This can be handy during the winter months when a hive requires additional feeding. To set scale 1 trigger-value to 30kg and scale 2 to 25kg send: Trigger 1=30kg, 2=25kg To disarm just set the trigger value to 0.

SMS-report

iBees can send you a daily SMS with the weight changes of the various scales over the last 24h. This makes it possible to follow up the changes without internet access. Unfortenately due to limited memory, daily reports are only possible with 30min or 60min intervals. Valid commands are: SMSreport ON, SMSreport OFF

Battery
Battery life

Both hub and scales are using 18650 rechargeable lithium battery's. Scale battery will last for a few years. Hub battery's will last about 6 months.

Low voltage

When scale or hub voltages drop to low, the system will send out a "Low voltage" warning. When the warning is ignored and the voltage drops below 3,55V scale or hub will eventually stop working. Caution: Do not fully discharge a lithium battery as they could be damaged!

Charging

Set the switch to OFF and remove the battery from its clips. A fully charged battery has an output of about 4,1V. A lithium battery can be over 500 times recharged.

FAQ
Communication cost

Monthly data upload for a standard 30min reading interval will be less than 1MB. Apart from the occasional alert, there will be normally no SMS communication (unless daily SMS reports are requested). Actual communication cost will of course vary per provider, but will be minimal.

GPRS network coverage

Most provider offer GPRS coverage everywhere. Check with your network provider if this includes your location.

Wireless scale range

Like any wifi connection, obstructions between scale and hub will reduce the communication range. For best reception, the hub should be placed in the upright position in the neighbourhood of the scales. Unobstructed scales will have a range of 50m.

More than 5 scales

At the moment the system is limited to maximum 5 scales per hub. If you wish to have more scales, just add another hub. Caution: to avoid radio interference, 2 hubs and its associated scales have to be 100m apart.

Working on hives

Working on a hive might trigger a swarm-alert or theft-alert. This can be avoided by disarming both alerts by sending an SMS on the previous day. Just switching the scale or hub to OFF without disarming it, might not prevent the alert. It will merely delay an alert until switch back ON.

Winter

When daily uploads are not needed, switch off scales and hub. Store them warm and dry place. Switching OFF only the hub, while leave the scales ON will quickly drain their battery's.
